3 TASK FORCE TO DEVELOP A TEN-YEAR PLAN AND FUNDING STRATEGY TO
4 ADDRESS MODERNIZATION AND SYSTEMIC PROBLEMS IN THE NEW MEXICO
5 CRIMINAL JUSTICE SYSTEM THAT INTERFERE WITH SYSTEM
6 PERFORMANCE, PUBLIC SAFETY AND EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW.
7
8 WHEREAS, Policy Spotlight: Felony Arrests and Outcomes,
9 a report by the legislative finance committee, examines
10 levels of serious crime in New Mexico in comparison to other
11 states; and
12 WHEREAS, Policy Spotlight: Felony Arrests and Outcomes
13 documents major impediments in the processing of individuals
14 arrested for crimes across law enforcement, district attorney
15 offices, the courts and corrections and reveals high rates of
16 case dismissals both prior to and at adjudication, resulting
in repeat offenses that often escalate in violence over time;
17
and
18
WHEREAS, Policy Spotlight: Felony Arrests and Outcomes
19
describes a lack of consistent reporting of crime, case
20
dismissals and case dispositions across the criminal justice
21
system, despite such reporting being required by law and
22
necessary for the legislature, the executive and the courts
23
to clearly identify justice system performance problems; and
24
WHEREAS, the New Mexico Law Enforcement Academy Job Task
25 HM 13/a
Page 1
1 and Curriculum Analysis final report published by the
2 international association of directors of law enforcement
3 external assessment under contract to the department of
4 public safety concluded that initial law enforcement
5 certification training is outdated and misaligned with
6 national standards, contributing to an undertrained public
7 safety workforce that is not consistently prepared to provide
8 scientifically valid evidence or respond effectively to
9 evolving criminal activity, and continuing education and
10 advanced and specialized training were found to be similarly
11 outdated; and
12 WHEREAS, training and education functions for criminal
13 justice system positions, currently located within operating
14 agencies, are understaffed and underfunded and should be
15 transferred to institutions of higher education to allow for
16 updated and enforceable standards; and
WHEREAS, program evaluation reports conducted by the
17
legislative finance committee have been shared with

19
indicates consistently high vacancy and turnover rates in
20
critical positions within the criminal justice system
21
statewide, exacerbating inadequate responses to community
22
needs, delays in case processing and adjudication and
23
challenges in safely managing corrections and community
24
reentry functions; and

1 WHEREAS, local agencies with significant vacancy and
2 turnover rates lack both the resources and expertise to
3 evaluate alternative staffing strategies and deploy effective
4 recruiting strategies; and
5 WHEREAS, justice system positions are largely shaped by
6 diverse local practices and, with limited local exceptions,
7 have not adapted to twenty-first-century challenges, failing
8 to provide clear career pathways to retain existing personnel
9 or attract talented youth and mid-career professionals and
10 contributing to the loss of experienced New Mexico
11 professionals to other states; and
12 WHEREAS, the lack of oversight for law enforcement,
13 prosecution, adjudication and corrections undermines public
14 confidence in the criminal justice system; and
15 WHEREAS, presentations by the New Mexico association of
16 counties, the New Mexico municipal league and other
stakeholders report high rates of litigation and settlement
17
costs for alleged civil rights violations across the criminal
18
justice system, posing a threat to the financial viability of
19
local governments;
20

8 consultation with the legislative finance committee staff

6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force's goals
7 include:
8 A. establishing a planning and oversight body to
9 integrate local planning into a state-level modernization
10 plan for the justice system and monitor implementation for at
11 least ten years;
12 B. establishing a process for modernizing justice
13 system position descriptions, educational requirements and
14 staffing adequacy;

21

22
E. identifying work groups and a process to define
23

1 whole;
2 F. establishing a process to plan for career paths
3 for justice system professionals, with linkage to the state
4 college system; and
5 G. recommending legislation for the 2027
6 legislative session to address the most immediate needs
7 related to these goals; and
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force be requested
9 to submit a report on its findings and recommendations to the
10 interim committee that studies courts, corrections and
11 justice issues by November 26, 2026 and by January 5, 2027 to
12 the governor and the legislature for consideration during the
13 first session of the fifty-eighth legislature; and
