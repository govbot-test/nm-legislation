Section 1:
1 A MEMORIAL
3 A TASK FORCE TO STUDY AND RECOMMEND A REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
4 FOR ACTIVITIES OR SERVICES OFFERED OR RENDERED BY PERSONS
5 OPERATING IN NEW MEXICO'S MOTION PICTURE, TELEVISION AND
6 PERFORMING ARTS INDUSTRIES.
7
8 WHEREAS, the motion picture, television and performing
9 arts industries are important components of New Mexico's
10 economy and workforce; and
11 WHEREAS, artists and performers rely on professional
12 services to obtain employment, develop careers, manage public
13 presence and negotiate compensation; and
14 WHEREAS, the term "talent management" is commonly used
15 to describe activities or professional services provided in
16 exchange for monetary compensation that include procuring or
attempting to procure for monetary compensation: employment,
17
career counseling or consulting, branding, publicity, social
18
media and digital presence management, contract-related
19
advising, referral services or casting-related services; and
20
WHEREAS, persons commonly known as "talent agents"
21
engage in procuring, offering, promising or attempting to
22
procure employment or engagements for artists or performers,
23
whether or not those persons are franchised by a labor
24
organization; and
25 HM 17
Page 1
1 WHEREAS, persons commonly known as "talent managers", in
2 exchange for monetary compensation, but without regulatory
3 standards, provide career guidance or representation and, in
4 some cases, engage directly or indirectly in the procurement
5 of employment; and
6 WHEREAS, certain casting entities, including background
7 casting companies and independent casting services not
8 affiliated with recognized professional associations, may
9 exercise discretion without uniform oversight over performer
10 selection or placement; and
11 WHEREAS, some additional services related to talent
12 management, including public relations, publicity, social
13 media management or digital brand representation, may overlap
14 with employment procurement or contractual arrangements; and
15 WHEREAS, the lack of regulatory standards and oversight
16 has led to an unclear understanding of industry terms,
creates uncertainty for artists or performers, service
17
providers and producers and may expose those in the industry
18
to unfair practices or exorbitant fees; and
19
WHEREAS, other states have enacted statutory frameworks
20
defining the titles and roles of talent agents, managers and
21
related services providers and providing oversight regarding
22
talent employment procurement; and
23
WHEREAS, New Mexico does not have a statutory framework
24
to define, delineate and, as needed, regulate industry
25 HM 17
Page 2
1 practices, but the state's motion picture, television and
2 performing arts industries could benefit from such a
3 framework; and
4 WHEREAS, a task force convened by the regulation and
5 licensing department could study and evaluate existing gaps,
6 review industry best practices and make recommendations
7 tailored to New Mexico's motion picture, television and
8 performing arts industries;
9 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF
10 REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO that the
11 regulation and licensing department be requested to convene a
12 task force by July 15, 2026 to:
13 A. study and recommend a regulatory framework for
14 activities or services offered or rendered by persons
15 operating in New Mexico's motion picture, television and
16 performing arts industries. The study should include
industry-related services, including talent management,
17
talent agents, talent managers, talent advisors, talent
18
consultants, publicists, social media managers or consultants
19
and background casting services and casting entities not
20
affiliated with the casting society;
21
B. evaluate when industry-related activities or
22
services constitute the procurement of employment and warrant
23
regulation or oversight by the regulation and licensing
24
department; and
25 HM 17
Page 3
1 C. recommend statutory or administrative action to
2 promote transparency, fairness and workforce protection while
3 supporting growth in New Mexico's motion picture, television
4 and performing arts industries; and
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force include:
6 A. a representative from the screen actors guild-
7 American federation of television and radio artists;
8 B. a representative from the New Mexico film
9 division of the economic development department;
10 C. a representative from the governor's council on
11 film and media industries;
12 D. a member of the New Mexico house of
13 representatives;
14 E. a member of the New Mexico senate;
15 F. a casting director accredited by the casting
16 society;
G. a talent agent franchised by the screen actors
17
guild-American federation of television and radio artists;
18
H. a representative from the department of
19
justice;
20
I. a representative from the Albuquerque film
21
office;
22
J. a representative from the Santa Fe film office;
23
K. a representative from film Las Cruces; and
24
L. a representative from the Indian affairs
25 HM 17
Page 4
1 department; and
2 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force complete its
3 report no later than November 1, 2026 and report its findings
4 and recommendations no later than November 30, 2026 to the
5 governor, the legislative interim committee that studies
6 courts, corrections and justice, the New Mexico film division
7 of the economic development department and the executive
8 director of the screen actors guild-American federation of
9 television and radio artists; and
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this memorial be
11 transmitted to the superintendent of regulation and licensing
12 and the governor. HM 17
