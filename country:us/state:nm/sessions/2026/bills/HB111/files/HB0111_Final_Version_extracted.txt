Section 1:
2 RELATING TO WATER; INCREASING THE MAXIMUM PENALTY FOR A
3 VIOLATION OF WATER LAW.
4
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO:
6 SECTION 1. Section 72-2-18 NMSA 1978 (being Laws 2001,
7 Chapter 143, Section 1, as amended) is amended to read:
8 "72-2-18. STATE ENGINEER--ENFORCEMENT--COMPLIANCE
9 ORDERS--PENALTY.--
10 A. When a person, pursuant to a finding of fact,
11 violates a requirement or prohibition of Chapter 72 NMSA
12 1978, a regulation, code, order or special order adopted by
13 the state engineer pursuant to Section 72-2-8 NMSA 1978, a
14 condition of a permit or license issued by the state engineer
15 pursuant to law or an order entered by a court adjudicating a
16 water right, the state engineer may, in addition to any other
17 remedies available under law, issue a compliance order
18 stating with reasonable specificity the nature of the
19 violation and requiring compliance within a specified time
period. A compliance order shall not be effective against a
20
party other than the person against whom the compliance order
21
is issued.
22
B. This section shall not be construed to affect
23
or interfere with any jurisdiction of an irrigation district,
24
a conservancy district, a state court, a federal court or an
25 HB 111
Page 1
1 Indian nation, tribe or pueblo to enforce its orders and
2 decrees pertaining to water rights.
3 C. A compliance order may include an order to
4 cease the violation of a requirement or prohibition of
5 Chapter 72 NMSA 1978, a directive issued in accordance with
6 the provisions of Section 72-2-8 NMSA 1978, a condition of a
7 permit or license issued by the state engineer or an order
8 entered by a court adjudicating a water right.
9 D. The state engineer shall provide an opportunity
10 for the person named in the compliance order to have a
11 hearing on the alleged violation pursuant to Section 72-2-16
12 NMSA 1978. A hearing shall be held if a written request is
13 made to the state engineer within thirty days after receipt
14 of the notice of the compliance order sent by certified mail.
15 If service by certified mail is not possible, the state
16 engineer may serve the compliance order by process server or
17 other effective means. Notice shall be deemed effective when
delivered to the address of record of the person named in the
18
compliance order. A compliance order issued pursuant to this

2 E. A compliance order may require repayment of
3 water that was overdiverted or illegally diverted. Repayment
4 of water may be up to double the amount of the overdiversion
5 or illegal diversion. In determining the repayment of water
6 beyond the amount overdiverted or illegally diverted, the
7 state engineer shall take into consideration the seriousness
8 of the violation, any good faith efforts to comply with the
9 applicable requirements and any other relevant factors. Any
10 requirement to repay water shall not be enforceable until the
11 compliance order becomes final. Installation of a measuring

1 dollars ($3,400) per day for each day during any portion of
2 which the violation continues. If the water is sold without
3 a permit to do so, the maximum penalty shall be double the
4 economic benefit to the violator resulting from the violation
5 or twenty-five dollars ($25.00) per barrel of water sold,
6 whichever is greater. In determining the amount of the civil
7 penalty, the state engineer shall take into consideration the
8 seriousness of the violation, any good faith efforts to
9 comply with the applicable requirements and any other
10 relevant factors. A compliance order may require payment of
11 the assessed civil penalty accruing from the date of the
12 notice of violation. The civil penalty shall not be
13 enforceable until the compliance order becomes final.
14 H. If a person does not comply with a compliance
15 order, the state engineer may file a civil action in the
16 county in which the violation occurred to enforce the
17 compliance order and receive any of the remedies provided in
this section, including injunctive relief."
