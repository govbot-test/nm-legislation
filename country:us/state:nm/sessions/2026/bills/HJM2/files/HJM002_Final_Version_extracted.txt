Section 1:
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
3 STUDY THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE INSPECTION OF PUBLIC RECORDS
4 ACT AND FOR THAT TASK FORCE TO GATHER INPUT FROM RELEVANT
5 STAKEHOLDERS TO DETERMINE WHETHER ADMINISTRATIVE SOLUTIONS OR
6 LEGISLATIVE CHANGES ARE NECESSARY OR DESIRABLE AND, IF SO, TO
7 PROPOSE SUCH LEGISLATION FOR CONSIDERATION DURING THE FIRST
8 SESSION OF THE FIFTY-EIGHTH LEGISLATURE.
9
10 WHEREAS, the Inspection of Public Records Act provides
11 the public with access to public records of government
12 entities; and
13 WHEREAS, Section 14-2-1 NMSA 1978 declares that the
14 policy of the state is that "[e]very person has a right to
15 inspect public records of this state", with some exceptions;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, providing access to public records promotes
18 transparency in the operations of government and the conduct
19 of public officials and employees and enables members of the
20 public to hold government actors accountable; and
21 WHEREAS, the legislature has amended the Inspection of
22 Public Records Act over time and should continue to evaluate
23 that act to identify and implement improvements that promote
24 clarity in its application, enhance transparency in
25 government and strengthen public trust in state institutions; HJC/HJM 2
Page 1
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE
2 STATE OF NEW MEXICO that the attorney general be requested to
3 convene a task force to study the implementation of the
4 Inspection of Public Records Act; gather input from relevant
5 stakeholders; evaluate that act's workability and
6 effectiveness in ensuring government transparency and serving
7 the public interest; and, if necessary or desirable, propose
8 administrative solutions or legislation based on the task
9 force's findings and recommendations for consideration during
10 the first session of the fifty-eighth legislature; and
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force consist of
12 one representative from each of the following: the
13 department of justice, the New Mexico municipal league, the
14 New Mexico association of counties, the New Mexico foundation
15 for open government, the American civil liberties union of
16 New Mexico, the New Mexico press association, a defense
17 attorney specializing in the Inspection of Public Records Act
18 and a plaintiff's attorney specializing in the Inspection of
19 Public Records Act; provided that the attorney general shall
20 appoint the defense attorney and plaintiff's attorney; and
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force adhere to the
22 Open Meetings Act and allow the public and other governmental
23 entities to provide input on the work of the task force,
24 including input from public schools and public school
25 districts; institutions of higher education; the judicial HJC/HJM 2
Page 2
1 branch of government, including district attorneys and public
2 defenders and their records custodians; and broadcast
3 journalists and other interested and affected parties; and
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force report its
5 recommendations to the appropriate interim committee that
6 deals with courts, corrections and justice and the
7 legislative finance committee by October 1, 2026; and
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this memorial be
9 transmitted to the attorney general. HJC/HJM 2
