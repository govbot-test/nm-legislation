7 SECTION 13. TEMPORARY PROVISIONS.--
8 A. For the purposes of the Public School Finance
9 Act in fiscal year 2026, a school district with MEM over
10 eight thousand whose MEM on the first reporting date of
11 fiscal year 2026 is at least ten percent lower than the
12 school district's MEM on the first reporting date of fiscal
13 year 2025 shall calculate the total number of program units
14 to which the school district is entitled by averaging the
15 school district's MEM on the first reporting date of fiscal
16 year 2025 and the school district's MEM on the first
17 reporting date of fiscal year 2026.
18 B. For the purposes of the Public School Finance
19 Act in fiscal years 2026 and 2027, a school district is not
20 eligible for enrollment growth program units pursuant to
21 Section 22-8-23.1 NMSA 1978 for students enrolled in a full-
22 time distance learning program.
23 C. For fiscal year 2027, a school district or
24 charter school shall not reorganize to create a school
25 district, school or school program that has a majority of HEC/HB 253/a/ec
Page 19
1 students enrolled in a full-time distance learning program.
2 D. The legislative education study committee, in
3 collaboration with the legislative finance committee and the
4 public education department, shall conduct a comprehensive
5 study on virtual instruction in the state. The study shall
6 evaluate the status of full-time distance learning programs
7 or similar programs and assess the outcomes of students
8 enrolled in the programs, with particular focus on those
9 students who are in kindergarten through fifth grade, the
10 cost of operating the programs and potential legislative
11 action regarding full-time distance learning programs. On or
12 before November 1, 2026, the legislative education study
13 committee shall submit a final report on the comprehensive
14 study to the governor, the public education department and
15 the legislative finance committee.
16 E. As used in this section, "full-time distance
17 learning program" means a public school instructional program
18 in which students receive virtual instruction and are not
19 required to attend school at a school building.
