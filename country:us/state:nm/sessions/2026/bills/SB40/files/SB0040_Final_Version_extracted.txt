Section 1:
2 RELATING TO LICENSE PLATE READERS; ENACTING THE DRIVER
3 PRIVACY AND SAFETY ACT; PROVIDING LIMITATIONS ON THE SHARING
4 OF AUTOMATED LICENSE PLATE READER INFORMATION AND REQUIRING
5 REPORTING; PROVIDING FOR ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES.
6
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO:
8 SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.--This act may be cited as the
9 "Driver Privacy and Safety Act".
10 SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS.--As used in the Driver Privacy
11 and Safety Act:
12 A. "automated license plate reader" means an
13 electronic device that is capable of recording data on or
14 taking a photograph of a vehicle or the vehicle's license
15 plate and comparing the collected data and photographs to
16 existing law enforcement databases for investigative purposes
17 and includes a device that is owned or operated by a person
18 or an entity other than a law enforcement agency;
19 B. "automated license plate reader information"
20 means information gathered by an automated license plate
21 reader or created from the analysis of data generated by an
22 automated license plate reader, excluding information used
23 for the purpose of auditing an automated license plate
24 reader;
25 C. "automated license plate reader user" means a SJC/SB 40
Page 1
1 person or an entity that owns or operates an automated
2 license plate reader or has access to automated license plate
3 reader information;
4 D. "law enforcement agency" means a state or local
5 agency, political subdivision of the state or private entity
6 that employs certified law enforcement officers or that is
7 charged with the enforcement of state, county or municipal
8 laws or with managing custody of detained persons in any
9 state or jurisdiction, including the police department of a
10 municipality, the sheriff's office of a county, the
11 New Mexico state police division of the department of public
12 safety, the department of public safety or the police
13 department of an Indian nation, tribe or pueblo that has
14 entered into an agreement with the department of public
15 safety pursuant to Section 29-1-11 NMSA 1978; and
16 E. "protected health care activity" means:
17 (1) seeking, providing or receiving
reproductive or gender-affirming health care; or
18
(2) assisting an individual who is seeking,
19
receiving or providing reproductive or gender-affirming
20
health care, including providing:
21
(a) information;
22
(b) transportation;
23
(c) lodging; or
24
(d) material support.
25 SJC/SB 40
Page 2
1 SECTION 3. AUTOMATED LICENSE PLATE READERS--LIMITATIONS
2 ON SHARING.--
3 A. An automated license plate reader user shall
4 not sell, share, allow access to or transfer automated
5 license plate reader information if the automated license
6 plate reader user has reasonable belief that the automated
7 license plate reader information may be used for the purpose
8 of:
9 (1) investigating or otherwise assisting in
10 immigration enforcement;
11 (2) investigating or prosecuting a protected
12 health care activity; or
13 (3) efforts to identify or impose civil or
14 criminal liability upon a person or an entity based solely on
15 the person's or entity's participation in activities
16 protected by the United States constitution or the
17 constitution of New Mexico, including assembly, petitioning
and speech.
18
B. An automated license plate reader user in
19
New Mexico that uses automated license plate reader systems
20
shall not allow access to automated license plate reader
21
information by an out-of-state third party or a
22
non-New Mexico law enforcement agency without first obtaining
23
from that party a written declaration that the party
24
expressly affirms that automated license plate reader
25 SJC/SB 40
Page 3
1 information obtained shall not be used in a manner that would
2 constitute a violation of Subsection A of this section. If a
3 written declaration of affirmation is not executed, the
4 automated license plate reader user shall not share the
5 automated license plate reader information with the third
6 party.
7 C. An automated license plate reader user shall
8 not sell, share, allow access to, transfer or otherwise make
9 available automated license plate reader information to
10 non-law enforcement officers or agencies. However, a user
11 may make available automated license plate reader information
12 to the user's automated license plate reader vendor for the
13 purpose of providing the automated license plate reader
14 service to the user.
15 D. Automated license plate reader information is
16 confidential and not considered a public record for the
17 purposes of the Inspection of Public Records Act.
E. Privately captured automated license plate
18
reader information may only be sold, shared, transferred or
19
released with the express consent of the private entity or
20
pursuant to a valid court order.
21

Section 2:
SECTION 4. AUTOMATED LICENSE PLATE READERS--
22
REPORTING.--
23
A. A law enforcement agency that uses an automated
24
license plate reader system shall report to the department of
25 SJC/SB 40
Page 4
1 public safety by April 1 of each year regarding the preceding
2 calendar year. The report shall include:
3 (1) the total number of cameras owned or
4 leased, categorized by mobile or stationary placement;
5 (2) a list of all state and federal
6 databases with which the system data was compared;
7 (3) the total number of times the system was
8 queried, including the purposes for the queries;
9 (4) a list of other law enforcement agencies
10 granted access to the system data;
11 (5) the number of identified instances of
12 unauthorized use or access to the system as determined by the
13 respective agency's audit policy;
14 (6) the number of subpoenas, search warrants
15 and third-party requests received for system data or audit
16 trails, including whether data was provided;
17 (7) the number of requests for automated
license plate reader information from out-of-state third
18
parties; and
19
(8) a list of all out-of-state agencies that
20
queried automated license plate reader data.
21
B. The department of public safety shall aggregate
22
reporting data and make that data publicly available by
23
July 1 of each year.
24

Section 3:
SECTION 5. ENFORCEMENT--PENALTIES.--
25 SJC/SB 40
Page 5
1 A. The attorney general or a district attorney is
2 authorized to enforce the provisions of the Driver Privacy
3 and Safety Act.
4 B. In an action to enforce the Driver Privacy and
5 Safety Act, the court may award appropriate relief, including
6 temporary, preliminary or permanent injunctive relief. The
7 court may also assess a civil penalty for an intentional
8 violation of Section 3 of the Driver Privacy and Safety Act
9 in the amount of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) or actual
10 damages resulting from each violation, whichever is greater.
11 C. The department of public safety is authorized
12 to enforce the provisions of the Driver Privacy and Safety
13 Act.
14 D. A law enforcement agency that fails to comply
15 with the Driver Privacy and Safety Act may have that law
16 enforcement agency's automated license plate reader data
17 sharing privileges revoked and access to the state-managed
automated license plate reader systems restricted.
18
E. A vendor of automated license plate readers
19
that fails to comply with the Driver Privacy and Safety Act
20
or department of public safety rules may be subject to
21
corrective action, suspension of authorization or termination
22
of access.
23

Section 4:
SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.--The effective date of the
24
25 provisions of this act is July 1, 2026. SJC/SB 40
Page 6
