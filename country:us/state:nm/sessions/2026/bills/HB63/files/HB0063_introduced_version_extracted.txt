HB0063 HOUSE BILL 63 57th legislature - STATE OF NEW MEXICO - second session, 2026 INTRODUCED BY Kathleen Cates and John Block and Pete Campos ENDORSED BY THE NEW MEXICO FINANCE AUTHORITY OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE AN ACT RELATING TO FINANCE; AUTHORIZING THE NEW MEXICO FINANCE AUTHORITY TO MAKE LOANS OR GRANTS FROM THE WATER PROJECT FUND FOR CERTAIN WATER PROJECTS; DECLARING AN EMERGENCY. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO: SECTION 1. [NEW MATERIAL] AUTHORIZATION OF QUALIFYING WATER PROJECTS.--Pursuant to Section 72-4A-9 NMSA 1978, the legislature authorizes the New Mexico finance authority to make loans or grants from the water project fund to the following qualifying entities for the following qualifying water projects on terms and conditions established by the water trust board and the New Mexico finance authority: 1. to the city of Gallup in McKinley county for one water conservation, treatment, recycling or reuse project, one water storage, conveyance and delivery project and one wastewater conveyance and treatment project; 2. to the Albuquerque-Bernalillo county water utility authority in Bernalillo county for one water conservation, treatment, recycling or reuse project and one water storage, conveyance and delivery project; 3. to the village of Los Ranchos de Albuquerque in Bernalillo county for one water conservation, treatment, recycling or reuse project and one flood prevention project; 4. to Luna county for a flood prevention project; 5. to the city of Sunland Park in Dona Ana county for one flood prevention project and two water storage, conveyance and delivery projects; 6. to Dona Ana county for a flood prevention project; 7. to the village of Ruidoso in Lincoln county for one flood prevention project, four water storage, conveyance and delivery projects and six wastewater conveyance and treatment projects; 8. to the southern Sandoval county arroyo flood control authority in Sandoval county for a flood prevention project; 9. to Lincoln county for a flood prevention project; 10. to the upper Rio Grande watershed district in Rio Arriba county for a flood prevention project;
