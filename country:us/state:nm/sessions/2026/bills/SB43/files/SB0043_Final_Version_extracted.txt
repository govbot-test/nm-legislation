9 SECTION 1. Section 31-21-10 NMSA 1978 (being Laws 1980,
10 Chapter 28, Section 1, as amended) is amended to read:
11 "31-21-10. PAROLE AUTHORITY AND PROCEDURE.--
12 A. Except as provided in Section 31-21-10.2
13 NMSA 1978, an inmate of an institution who was sentenced to
14 life imprisonment becomes eligible for a parole hearing after
15 the inmate has served thirty years of the sentence, which
16 shall be construed as the retributive portion of the life
17 sentence. Upon reaching eligibility, parole consideration
18 for release shall focus on:
19 (1) risk and readiness for release, as
20 demonstrated by the inmate substantially complying with the
21 rules of the institution to which the inmate has been
22 confined;
23 (2) whether the inmate has participated in
24 or completed an educational, a vocational or another program,
25 where available, while confined; and SB 43

1 (3) whether the inmate has demonstrated
2 maturity, rehabilitation and a fitness to reenter society.
3 B. Before ordering the parole of an inmate
4 sentenced to life imprisonment, the board shall:
5 (1) interview the inmate at the institution
6 where the inmate is committed;
7 (2) hear from the family or representative
8 of the victim, if the family or representative chooses to
9 participate;
10 (3) consider all pertinent information
11 concerning the inmate, including:
12 (a) the circumstances of the offense,
13 including mitigating and aggravating circumstances;
14 (b) whether a deadly weapon was used in
15 the commission of the offense;
16 (c) the inmate's relevant criminal
17 history;
(d) the reports filed pursuant to

19

20
mental examinations as have been made while the inmate was in

an institution;
22
(4) make a finding that a parole is in the
23
best interest of society and the inmate; and
24
(5) make a finding that the inmate is able

1 and willing to fulfill the obligations of a law-abiding
2 citizen.
3 C. If parole is denied, the inmate sentenced to
4 life imprisonment shall again become entitled to a parole
5 hearing at two-year intervals. The board may, on its own
6 motion, reopen any case in which a hearing has already been
7 granted and parole denied.
8 D. Unless the board finds that it is in the best
9 interest of society and the parolee to reduce the period of
10 parole, a person who was sentenced to life imprisonment shall
11 be required to undergo a minimum period of parole of five
12 years. During the period of parole, the person shall be
13 under the guidance and supervision of the board.
14 E. An inmate of an institution who was sentenced
15 to life imprisonment without possibility of release or parole
16 is not eligible for parole and shall remain incarcerated for
17 the entirety of the inmate's natural life.

SECTION 3. Section 31-21-24 NMSA 1978 (being Laws 1975,

Chapter 194, Section 3, as amended) is amended to read:
25 SB 43
Page 5
1 "31-21-24. PAROLE BOARD--MEMBERS--APPOINTMENT--TERMS--
2 QUALIFICATIONS--COMPENSATION--ORGANIZATION.--
3 A. The "parole board" is created, consisting of
4 fifteen members appointed by the governor with the consent of
5 the senate.
6 B. The terms of the members of the parole board
7 shall be six years. To provide for staggered terms, five
8 members shall be appointed every two years. Members serve
9 until their successors have been appointed and qualified.
10 C. Members of the parole board may be removed by
11 the governor as provided in Article 5, Section 5 of the
12 constitution of New Mexico. Vacancies shall be filled by
13 appointment by the governor for the remainder of the
14 unexpired term.
15 D. Members of the parole board shall be persons
16 qualified by such academic training or professional
17 experience as is deemed necessary to render them fit to serve
as members of the board. No member of the board shall be an
18
official or employee of any other federal, state or local
19
government entity.
20
E. Members of the parole board shall receive per
21
diem and mileage as provided for nonsalaried public officers
22
in the Per Diem and Mileage Act for a scheduled board meeting
23
or hearing or any other reimbursable activity under that act
24
and shall receive no other compensation, perquisite or
25 SB 43
Page 6
1 allowance.
2 F. The governor shall designate one member of the
3 parole board to serve as chair, who in addition to other
4 duties shall coordinate with the corrections department
5 in the furnishing of services pursuant to Section 9-3-11
6 NMSA 1978.
7 G. A parole may be granted, denied or revoked by a
8 quorum of two on a panel consisting of three parole board
9 members appointed on a rotating basis by the chair of the
10 board."
11 SECTION 4. A new section of the Parole Board Act,
12 Section 31-21-25.2 NMSA 1978, is enacted to read:
13 "31-21-25.2. SCHEDULING OF HEARINGS IN CASES OF
14 HOMICIDE.--In cases of homicide, the parole board shall not
15 schedule a hearing on the anniversary of the birth or death of
16 the person or persons whose death is the basis for the
17 homicide conviction, when practicable." SB 43
