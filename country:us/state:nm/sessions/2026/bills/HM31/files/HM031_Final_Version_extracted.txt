1 A MEMORIAL
3 HOME HEALTH SERVICE DISTANCE LIMITATIONS TO IMPROVE ACCESS TO
4 CARE IN RURAL, FRONTIER AND TRIBAL COMMUNITIES.
5
6 WHEREAS, home health agencies play a critical role in
7 delivering medically necessary skilled nursing, therapy and
8 supportive services that allow New Mexico residents to
9 receive care in their homes rather than in institutional
10 settings; and
11 WHEREAS, the health care authority's current
12 administrative rules limit a home health agency to serving
13 patient-clients who reside within one hundred miles of the
14 agency's licensed location; and
15 WHEREAS, this limitation creates administrative and
16 operational burdens for home health agencies, particularly
those serving large geographic areas with sparse populations,
17
limited infrastructure and workforce shortages; and
18
WHEREAS, the reauthorization of the federal Radiation
19
Exposure Compensation Act is expected to increase demand for
20
home health services among uranium miners and millers, many
21
of whom reside in remote frontier areas and on Navajo Nation
22
tribal lands; and
23
WHEREAS, these communities already face significant
24
barriers to accessing health care services due to geographic
25 HM 31
Page 1
1 isolation, transportation challenges and limited availability
2 of providers; and
3 WHEREAS, the current distance-based limitation on home
4 health services creates a regulatory barrier that may
5 unintentionally limit access to medically necessary care for
6 eligible patients in these underserved regions; and
7 WHEREAS, the personal care services program administered
8 by the health care authority allows providers to serve
9 patients in any county within one hundred miles of a
10 provider's main or branch office, offering a more flexible
11 and access-oriented regulatory framework; and
12 WHEREAS, aligning the health care authority's rules
13 governing home health service with existing personal care
14 service policies would promote consistency across programs
15 while maintaining appropriate oversight and quality
16 standards;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF
17
REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO that the health
18
care authority be requested to reevaluate the administrative
19
rules governing home health agency service areas, including
20
the rule that limits a home health agency to serving
21
patient-clients who reside within one hundred miles of the
22
agency's licensed location; and
23
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that this reevaluation prioritize
24
improving access to home health services for rural, frontier
25 HM 31
Page 2
1 and tribal communities, including populations impacted by
2 radiation exposure; and
3 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this memorial be
4 transmitted to the secretary of health care authority, the
5 governor and the legislative health and human services
6 committee. HM 31
