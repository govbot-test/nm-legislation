Section 1:
HB0317 HOUSE BILL 317 57th legislature - STATE OF NEW MEXICO - second session, 2026 INTRODUCED BY Jenifer Jones and Luis M. Terrazas and Gabriel Ramos AN ACT RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES; ESTABLISHING THE SPECIAL PERMIT REVIEW BOARD; CREATING A MISDEMEANOR FOR THE OPERATION OF AN OVERWEIGHT VEHICLE IN VIOLATION OF TIME SPECIFIED IN A SPECIAL PERMIT ISSUED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION OR LOCAL HIGHWAY AUTHORITIES; PROVIDING AN OPPORTUNITY FOR A HEARING; PROVIDING CIVIL PENALTIES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO: SECTION 1. A new section of the Motor Vehicle Code is enacted to read: "[NEW MATERIAL] SPECIAL PERMIT REVIEW BOARD CREATED.-- A. The "special permit review board" is established within the department of transportation, composed of: (1) the secretary of public safety or the secretary's designee; (2) the secretary of transportation or the secretary's designee; and (3) the managing director of the New Mexico trucking association or the managing director's designee. B. The special permit review board shall meet as necessary to review applications by vehicle operators who are otherwise ineligible pursuant to Section 66-8-116.1 NMSA 1978 to receive special permits pursuant to the Motor Vehicle Code for continued eligibility to receive such permits. C. The department of transportation shall establish the criteria and procedures for the review of applications for continued eligibility to receive special permits pursuant to this section by the special permit review board by rule." SECTION 2. A new section of the Motor Vehicle Code, Section 66-7-413.9 NMSA 1978, is enacted to read: "66-7-413.9. [NEW MATERIAL] PERMITS FOR EXCESSIVE SIZE AND WEIGHT--VIOLATION OF TIME.--Except as a result of a mechanical failure or other circumstance outside of an operator's control, it is a misdemeanor to operate or move a vehicle pursuant to a special permit issued by the department of transportation or local highway authorities pursuant to Sections 66-7-412 through 66-7-413.8 NMSA 1978 in violation of a time specified in the permit for such operation or movement on a specified highway." SECTION 3. Section 66-8-116.1 NMSA 1978 (being Laws 1989, Chapter 319, Section 12, as amended) is amended to read: "66-8-116.1. PENALTY ASSESSMENT MISDEMEANORS--OVERSIZE LOAD.-- A. As used in the Motor Vehicle Code and the Motor Carrier Act, "penalty assessment misdemeanor" means, in addition to the definition of that term in Section 66-8-116 NMSA 1978, violation of the following listed sections of the NMSA 1978 for which, except as provided in Subsection B of this section, the listed penalty is established: COMMON NAME OF OFFENSE     SECTION VIOLATED     PENALTY                                                   ASSESSMENT Oversize load 1,000 to 3,000 pounds      66-7-411              $ 50.00 Oversize load 3,001 to 4,000 pounds      66-7-411                80.00 Oversize load 4,001 to 5,000 pounds      66-7-411               150.00 Oversize load 5,001 to 6,000 pounds      66-7-411               250.00 Oversize load 6,001 to 7,000 pounds      66-7-411               400.00 Oversize load 7,001 to 8,000 pounds      66-7-411               550.00 Oversize load 8,001 to 9,000 pounds      66-7-411               700.00 Oversize load 9,001 to 10,000 pounds     66-7-411               850.00 Oversize load over 10,000 pounds         66-7-411 1,000.00 First violation of time    66-7-413.9         1,000.00 Second violation of time   66-7-413.9         2,000.00. B. Upon a third violation of a special permit issued by the department of transportation to operate or move a vehicle or load pursuant to Sections 66-7-412 through 66-7-413.8 NMSA 1978, an operator shall not be eligible to receive a subsequent special permit for the movement of an oversized or overweight vehicle or load pursuant to the Motor Vehicle Code; provided that the operator may apply to the special permit review board in accordance with Section 1 of this 2026 act for continued eligibility to receive such permits." SECTION 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.--The effective date of the provisions of this act is July 1, 2026. - 4 -
