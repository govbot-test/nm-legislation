1 A MEMORIAL
3 ADDRESS THE NURSING SHORTAGE CRISIS.
4
5 WHEREAS, according to a study conducted by the workforce
6 solutions department in April 2025, there are nearly eighteen
7 thousand registered nurses, one thousand three hundred
8 licensed practical nurses and eight thousand three hundred
9 advanced practice nurses currently practicing in the state of
10 New Mexico; and
11 WHEREAS, nursing is the largest sector of the health
12 care workforce, yet there is a critical shortage of nurses in
13 the state, which is taxing on the state's health care
14 delivery systems and on access to care for state residents;
15 and
16 WHEREAS, this shortage is exacerbated by a gap in
education to meet workforce needs, and this gap is made clear
17
by the thousands of nursing job postings each month according
18
to the workforce solutions department; and
19
WHEREAS, even though appropriations in recent fiscal
20
years have been provided to fund additional nursing school
21
slots and faculty positions, rates of enrollment and degree
22
completion in nursing programs are not increasing as needed;
23
and
24
WHEREAS, approximately three thousand two hundred new
25 HM 36
Page 1
1 nurses need to graduate from nursing programs every year to
2 meet intended nurse staffing needs by 2032, but approximately
3 one thousand four hundred new nurses graduated in 2025;
4 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF
5 REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF NEW MEXICO that the board of
6 nursing be requested to create a task force to identify
7 barriers to increasing nursing graduation rates and employee
8 retention consisting of one member from each of the following
9 organizations:
10 A. nursing professional organizations, including
11 the New Mexico nurses association, the New Mexico nurse
12 practitioner council, the New Mexico organization of nurse
13 leaders and nursing unions;
14 B. the New Mexico hospital association;
15 C. the New Mexico nursing education consortium;
16 D. New Mexico colleges of nursing deans or
delegates;
17
E. post-secondary educational institutions; and
18
F. high school licensed practical nurse programs;
19
and
20
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force be requested
21
to report findings and recommendations to the appropriate
22
interim legislative committee that addresses health and human
23
services and the legislative finance committee and that the
24
report include:
25 HM 36

1 A. barriers to employment retention;
2 B. barriers to nursing educational slot expansion;
3 C. barriers to nursing school enrollment and
4 retention; and
5 D. recommendations to improve the nursing
6 educational gap to address nursing workforce needs; and
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this memorial be
8 transmitted to the board of nursing, the appropriate interim
9 legislative committee that addresses health and human services
